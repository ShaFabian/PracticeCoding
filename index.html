Understanding Types of Analytics

Descriptive Analytics Diagnostic Analytics Predictive Analytics

Descriptive Analytics

Descriptive analytics focuses on summarizing historical data to identify trends and patterns. It provides insights into what has happened in the past by organizing data into meaningful contexts. Common tools include dashboards and reports.

  • Purpose: To understand past performance.
  • Examples: Revenue reports, website traffic summaries.
  • Challenges:Data Overload,Data Accuracy and Consistency,Limited Insights,Timeliness .

Diagnostic Analytics

Diagnostic analytics digs deeper into data to determine the causes of events or trends. It helps answer the question, "Why did this happen?" by using techniques like drill-down, data discovery, and correlations.

  • Purpose: To identify root causes of outcomes.
  • Examples: Analyzing why sales dropped in a particular region.
  • Challenges:Identifying Root Causes,Data Granularity,Complexity of Analysis,Human Bias.

Predictive Analytics

Predictive analytics uses statistical models and machine learning techniques to forecast future outcomes based on historical data. It helps organizations anticipate trends and make proactive decisions.

  • Purpose: To predict future trends and behaviors.
  • Examples: Customer churn prediction, sales forecasting.
  • Challenges:Data Quality and Availability,Model Complexity,Changing Environments,Interpretation.
